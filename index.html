Simple Harmonic Oscillator: Ball on a spring

Simple Harmonic Oscillator

An example of an every day simple harmonic oscillator is a spring. A spring at rest, meaning neither compressed or stretched is at its equilibrium position. A spring will resist being displaced from this position as it is at its very lowest energy. When pulled away or compressed from its equilibrium position, the spring increases in its potential energy. An observer can witness energy when the spring is released from its stretched or compressed state. When released from this state, the spring will then oscillate between compressed and stretched. What happens to this back and forth motion over time? Why?

Try stretching and compressing the spring to the left. Observe the relationship between the distance from the springs equilibrium position and changes to the potential energy. What else do you notice? Try stretching the spring as far to the right as possible. What happens?
